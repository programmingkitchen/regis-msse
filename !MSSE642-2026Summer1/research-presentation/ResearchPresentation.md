## Research Presentation General Instructions. 
- Perform research on an topic related to AI and Software Engineering. 
- The focus does not have to be security.
- You can use AI to perform the research, but verify anything that AI tells you with sources.  You can have AI give you the sources and then you can read them. 
- You can talk about recent events and use non-academic sources. 

## Example topic ideas (pick a topic of your own)

### AI and Software Security Presentation Topics
- AI Coding Assistants and Secure Programming — How tools like GitHub change software development and introduce new security risks.
- Prompt Injection Attacks — How attackers manipulate AI systems through malicious prompts and hidden instructions.
- AI-Generated Malware — How criminals can use AI to automate malware creation and cyberattacks.
- Deepfakes and Identity Fraud — How AI-generated voices and videos threaten authentication and trust.
- AI in Cyber Defense — How AI is being used for intrusion detection, threat hunting, and incident response.
- Bias in AI Systems — How biased training data can create unfair or dangerous software behavior.
- Privacy Risks of Large Language Models — How AI systems may expose personal or confidential information.
- Adversarial Machine Learning — How attackers trick AI models using specially crafted inputs.
- AI and Phishing Attacks — How generative AI improves social engineering and scam effectiveness.
- AI Agents and Autonomous Decision Making — Security concerns around AI systems acting independently.
- Security Risks of Open-Source AI Models — Risks of downloading and running public AI models.
- AI and Software Supply Chain Security — How AI tools affect dependencies, libraries, and code trust.
- AI in Vulnerability Discovery — Using AI to automatically find software bugs and security flaws.
- Can AI Replace Human Programmers? — Security and reliability concerns with AI-generated software.
- AI Hallucinations in Critical Systems — The danger of incorrect AI outputs in healthcare, finance, or infrastructure.
- AI and Critical Infrastructure Security — How AI affects power grids, telecom, transportation, and utilities.
- Voice Cloning and Security — How cloned voices can bypass identity verification systems.
- AI and Surveillance Technology — Facial recognition, tracking systems, and privacy concerns.
- The Environmental Impact of AI — Energy use and infrastructure demands of large AI systems.
- AI Regulation and Government Policy — How governments are attempting to regulate AI technologies.
- Quantum Computing and AI Security — How future computing advances may affect encryption and AI systems.
- AI in Robotics Security — Security risks in autonomous robots and smart machines.
- Jailbreaking Large Language Models — Techniques users employ to bypass AI safety controls.
- AI and Fake News — How AI-generated content impacts misinformation and public trust.
- AI in Password Cracking — How AI accelerates credential attacks and brute-force techniques.
- Data Poisoning Attacks on AI Models — How attackers corrupt AI training datasets.
- AI and Zero-Day Exploits — Whether AI can discover unknown vulnerabilities faster than humans.
- Secure Development of AI Applications — Best practices for safely building AI-powered software.
- AI and Intellectual Property Theft — Legal and security concerns around training data and generated content.
- The Future of AI and Cyber Warfare — How nations may use AI in offensive and defensive cyber operations.
